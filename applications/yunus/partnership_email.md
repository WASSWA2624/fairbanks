**Subject:** Social business partnership proposal from FairBanks Medical Centre, Uganda

Dear Yunus Centre Team,

I hope you are well.

My name is Racheal Nabukeera, Managing Director of FairBanks Medical Centre in Kampala, Uganda. I am writing to explore a possible partnership with the Yunus Centre around social business approaches to community health, financial protection, and livelihoods for underserved families.

FairBanks runs three connected parts of work: our medical centre, FairBanks Community Reach (outreach, health education, prevention, CHIS, and livelihoods), and FCHIP — the FairBanks Community Health Intelligence Platform. We are developing an FCHIP MVP so community and facility teams can see health risks earlier and act sooner.

Professor Yunus’s social business idea — solving social problems through sustainable business methods — closely matches how we want FairBanks to grow: not as a charity-only model, and not as profit-first healthcare, but as a community health enterprise that keeps care close to home and helps families stay resilient.

We would welcome a conversation about areas such as:

- Community health as a social business in Uganda and East Africa
- CHIS and livelihood pathways as practical financial protection
- Learning from Grameen and Yunus Centre experience in health and social enterprise
- Advice and useful introductions while we develop the FCHIP MVP
- Possible exchange with the wider Yunus Social Business Centre (YSBC) network

I have attached a short partnership brief (PDF) with more detail on our model, current work, and how a collaboration might look.
