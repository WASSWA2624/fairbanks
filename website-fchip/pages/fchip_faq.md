# FCHIP — FAQ (website accordion)

### What is FCHIP?
FCHIP means **FairBanks Community Health Intelligence Platform**. It is FairBanks’ technology component that helps Community Reach and the Medical Centre see health risks earlier using community data, facility signals, maps, and climate information.

### Is FCHIP a replacement for the Medical Centre or Community Reach?
No. FairBanks remains a community health ecosystem: Community Reach, the Medical Centre, CHIS, and livelihoods. FCHIP is the **Data & Feedback** intelligence layer on that model.

### Who uses FCHIP?
CHWs and VHTs in the field, FairBanks Medical Centre teams, programme managers, and (as we scale) district partners, NGOs, and research collaborators.

### What data does it use?
Structured data from CHWs/VHTs, schools, community programmes, clinics and hospitals, secure facility system feeds where connected, pharmacies where available, and climate / weather APIs — always with privacy and consent rules in mind.

### Does a clinic need to change its EMR or hospital software?
No. FCHIP is designed to expose secure data APIs so existing EMR/HMS systems can share clinical data into the platform without replacing what facilities already use.

### Is FCHIP ready now?
A working MVP exists. We are validating it inside FairBanks’ live Community Reach cascade before wider district and regional scale-up.

### How does climate fit in?
Climate feeds (for example rainfall and heat) are fused with community and facility health signals and GIS maps so teams can act on climate-linked risk earlier — such as fever clusters after heavy rain.

### How can partners support FCHIP?
Co-fund CHIS, sponsor CHW/VHT training, fund health camps, support school health, join research, or strengthen the Data & Feedback / FCHIP layer. Contact info@fairbanksmedicalcentre.org.
